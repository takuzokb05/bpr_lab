## 投稿内容
Someone asked r/ProductManagement if anyone uses Claude Code for strategy work.

40 comments of pure fury.

Junior PM: I read that thread and I can't sleep. If AI can do strategy, what am I even here for?

Senior PM: Which part of strategy are you worried about losing?

Junior PM: Competitive analysis, opportunity sizing, writing PRDs. AI handles all of that now.

Senior PM: Try an exercise. Open Claude right now and ask it to prioritize your Q3 roadmap.

Junior PM: I just tried it and it gave me a decent framework with six criteria and a scoring matrix.

Senior PM: Does that output match what your team should build next quarter?

Junior PM: It doesn't, because it has no idea our CTO wants to migrate platforms, or that our biggest customer threatened to churn last week, or that we only have two senior engineers until September.

Senior PM: So the gap between that output and a real strategy is the 50 things you carry in your head about your company, your team, and your users.

Junior PM: Right, it's the context. The team dynamics, the technical debt, what our CEO said about shifting focus in the last all-hands. None of that made it into the prompt.

Senior PM: When people in that Reddit thread said "sure, sloppify the most important part of the job," I think they were reacting to PMs who paste a vague prompt and treat the output as strategy. And they're right to be frustrated by that.

Junior PM: So should I refuse AI and go back to doing quarterly planning manually over three weeks?

Senior PM: You could, and most of that thread would agree with you. But here's the exercise I'd suggest instead. Step one: write down your company's top three goals for the year in one page. Step two: list your five biggest product constraints, things like technical debt, team size, platform dependencies. Step three: document what your last ten user interviews told you about the biggest pain points. Those three documents take maybe two hours to write. Then feed them to AI alongside every strategic question.

Junior PM: So instead of AI replacing my judgment, it runs on top of my judgment because it has my context.

Senior PM: And the output goes from generic consulting-firm advice to recommendations that already account for your engineering constraints and your CEO's priorities. I think most PMs could cut their quarterly planning from three weeks of data gathering down to four or five days of making judgment calls, because the synthesis part is handled.

Junior PM: That's the opposite of what the Reddit thread assumes people are doing with AI.

Senior PM: I think both camps are wrong. The refusers are leaving a 3x to 5x speed multiplier on the table. The blind delegators are producing generic slop that any intern could spot. The PMs building context systems right now, the ones documenting their company knowledge in structured files and feeding it to every AI interaction, those are the ones who will have a clear edge in 12 months. Your competitive advantage was never analysis speed. It was always judgment applied to context that only you have.

That context system is what PM OS gives you.

Now V2 comes packed with a memory system influenced heavily by Hermes and OpenClaw agents.

Takes about 15 minutes to set everything up, and it cuts strategic planning time by 80% going forward.
