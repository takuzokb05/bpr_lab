## 投稿内容
Microsoft just put AI agents on the Windows taskbar.

Not Copilot chat. Not a sidebar assistant. Actual agents that live in your taskbar, powered by Model Context Protocol.

Same MCP that runs my entire operations stack. Same protocol Anthropic open-sourced. Same one that crossed 97 million installs three weeks ago.

Now it's baked into the operating system.

Here's what most people are missing about this:

The protocol war is over. MCP won. And the distribution game just shifted from "which model is best" to "which protocol connects everything."

Microsoft isn't building the agents. They're building the harness. Third-party developers plug their agents into MCP, connect to the Windows taskbar through a new API, and users interact with them like any other app.

Sound familiar?

That's the same pattern playing out everywhere. The model is the horse. The harness is the product. The person who knows which horse to ride and where to aim it is the operator.

Every major platform is converging on the same architecture: give agents a persistent identity, connect them via MCP, let them act across your tools.

Google just did the same thing with Gemini Enterprise. Cryptographic agent IDs. Agent Registry. Agent Gateway. All built on MCP and A2A.

The companies still debating which chatbot to subscribe to are already two moves behind.

The ones building agent infrastructure into their daily operations are going to look obvious in hindsight.

What's the first agent you'd pin to your taskbar?
