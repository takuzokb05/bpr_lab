## 投稿内容
TSMC just announced 70 percent annual growth in 2nm chip capacity through 2028. Every major AI company is fighting for allocation. This is why India's semiconductor timing matters.

When TSMC is capacity-constrained at the leading edge, the rest of the supply chain becomes critical. Packaging, testing, assembly, older-node fabs — this is exactly where India is building. Micron ATMP in Sanand. Tata OSAT in Assam. CG Semi packaging in Gujarat.

India is not competing for 2nm. India is positioning itself as the second layer of the global semiconductor supply chain — the layer that every 2nm chip eventually passes through for packaging and integration.

The global chip industry will be worth 1 trillion dollars by 2030. India's target is 100 billion in domestic semiconductor revenue. Even at 10 percent of the global market, that is transformational for Indian manufacturing.

TSMC's capacity crunch is India's opportunity window. When the world's largest chipmaker cannot meet demand, supply chain diversification stops being optional. India is the only democracy with scale, engineering talent, and government capital offering an alternative.
