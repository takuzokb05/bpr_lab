## 投稿内容
Someone needs to study how Anthropic can ship so fast AND run experiments at the same time.

They just created Project Deal, which has Claude negotiating real deals between employees.

> Built a Craigslist-style marketplace inside their SF office 69 employees brought in personal items (snowboards, bikes, electronics, ping-pong balls)

> Each got a $100 budget, and then handed everything to Claude.

> The agents posted listings. made offers. countered. walked away from bad deals. 

> Came back with better ones and closed transactions. all through Slack, zero human intervention.

> 186 deals closed. $4,000+ in total value. 500+ items listed.

> They ran 4 parallel markets. two used only Opus (the expensive model). two mixed Opus with Haiku (the cheap model).

Opus crushed it. Sellers using Opus got $3.64 more per deal on average and buyers paid $2.45 less. Across the board, the stronger model extracted better outcomes.

There's a few conclusions from this:

a) Companies need to be tokenmaxxing with the strongest models. If you're not using the most state-of-the-art models you'll lose to those who do

b) Companies which figure out agent quality through evals and building the right measurement loops will get much more out of their AI

c) Agent-native marketplaces will unlock a new efficient marketplaces in many verticals cc @tkexpress11's "Come for the Agent, Stay for the Network" post
