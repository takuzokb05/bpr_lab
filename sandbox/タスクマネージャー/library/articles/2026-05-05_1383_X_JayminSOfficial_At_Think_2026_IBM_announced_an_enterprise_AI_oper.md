## 投稿内容
Everyone's racing to build better AI models.

@IBM just bet the real money sits in controlling how those models work 𝙩𝙤𝙜𝙚𝙩𝙝𝙚𝙧.

At Think 2026, @IBMData skipped the foundation model announcement entirely. https://t.co/q5jluvAPY0 They announced an operating model, a software layer connecting agents, data, automation, and hybrid infrastructure across the entire enterprise.

That's a fundamentally different kind of ambition. And a smarter one.

Arvind Krishna put it plainly: "Over 70% of all the data is still sitting inside the enterprise. You have to act where the data is."

Rob Thomas, IBM's SVP of Software, took it further: "We now have a catalog of hundreds of agents provided by other companies." The focus has shifted from building agents to managing them across every system an enterprise touches.

This is where the real gap shows up. Enterprises have AI pilots. They lack AI systems. One solves a problem. The other transforms how a business runs, top to bottom.

Patrick Moorhead chief analyst at Moor Insights & Strategy, noted that: "Cloud agents don't run or manage on-premises agents. With more than 75% of enterprise data still on-premises or at the edge, you need an orchestrator that operates across cloud, on-prem and the mainframe."

Companies getting this right are seeing 40% productivity gains AND entirely new revenue streams from AI-embedded workflows. IBM's projections show 60% of value emerging from new business models, far beyond cost savings alone.

The hyperscalers build agentic capabilities inside their own sandboxes. Enterprise infrastructure lives everywhere. Whoever controls the layer connecting all of them controls the outcome.

That's the actual race. And IBM just staked their entire future on winning it.
